# Timeline — Hollowcrown

Approximate dates, marked by the **YS** calendar (*Years Since the Sundering*). The current game opens at **YS 198** — almost two centuries after the binding.

---

## Pre-Sundering

**~YS −300:**
The throne kingdom is founded. Its first king dies normally. So do his sons, his sons' sons, and so on for nine generations. The kingdom is unremarkable.

**~YS −200 to −5:**
The reign of the king-who-would-not-die. He takes the throne at a respectable age, fights two wars with his neighbors, and loses both. He does not abdicate. After his hundredth year, the court stops counting his birthdays. After his hundred and fiftieth, his court stops *being* his court — those who knew him as a young man are dust. New courtiers serve the king they have always served.

By **YS −20**, the king is no longer described as a man in any letter that survives. He has stopped issuing new edicts. He has stopped meeting petitioners. He sits the throne. The throne does not let him leave it.

The kingdoms surrounding his stop sending envoys. They have been sending them for two hundred years and getting nothing back.

## The Sundering — YS 0

The **Sealing Order** — twelve mages drawn from a dozen disciplines, lead by **Yorath Tellan-Brenn** — performs the binding.

The work takes nineteen days and twenty nights. It cannot be stopped once begun. Three of the twelve mages do not survive it. **Aelia Ren**, the cartographer-sealer, lives. So does Yorath.

The ritual splits the king into four:

1. **The Crown** — his body. Bound at the bottom of the Throne Beneath, under the actual throne. Wrapped in iron and bone.
2. **The Pact** — his oaths and unspoken promises. Bound under salt water in the Drowned Sanctum, where a Warden the Order builds for the purpose holds them silent.
3. **The Memory** — his name and his face from before the throne. Hidden in the Voidrift, the gap between what is and what is not. Inaccessible by intent.
4. **The Will** — the empty chooser. Left on the throne, crownless, alone. They could find no way to bind it that did not unbind the others.

The shape is closed.

The world is broken in the closing. Magic begins to leak from the edges of what was sealed. The kingdoms that survive call this the Sundering.

## Post-Sundering

**YS 1–10:**
The surviving Sealing Order disperses. They are not heroes — they are quiet, exhausted people who go home. Most refuse to teach what they did. The records of the binding are intentionally destroyed by the Order itself. **Yorath Tellan-Brenn** keeps a single notebook, which she hides with her family. Aelia Ren leaves journal pages and waystones across the lands she mapped during the ritual; she does not consider them dangerous.

**YS 12:**
Yorath dies of old age. Her notebook passes to her sister.

**YS 30–60:**
Magic-leak fauna emerge in the seam-zones. Existing kingdoms cannot field standing armies large enough to handle them. The first **Adventurer's Guilds** form in border towns to take the work the soldiers cannot. The Ashenvale guild is one of the earliest.

**YS 80–120:**
The Throne Beneath develops a reputation. Travelers who go too deep do not come back. Locals stop calling it by its old name. It becomes "the cairn" — the catacombs that grew up around the throne over generations. The Sealing Order's work is mostly forgotten. The four-fold seal is not on any map.

**YS 150:**
The Drowned Warden is heard for the first time in two centuries. A diver from a coastal village hears him speak under the water. The diver does not return. The story is dismissed.

**YS 188:**
A Hollow King — *not* the original; the first one to wear what was left of the Crown — appears in the Throne Beneath. He is killed by a guild expedition. His body is recovered. Brenna is twenty-two. Her aunt is Guildmaster.

**YS 195:**
Brenna becomes Guildmaster of Ashenvale on her aunt's death. She inherits the notebook in the same week. She reads it once. She does not open it again until YS 198.

**YS 196:**
The seal at the Throne Beneath fails for the third time. A new Hollow King wears the Crown — a fallen guildmaster's body, this time, taken before burial. The pattern is starting to show.

**YS 197:**
The Drowned Warden begins answering questions asked of him through the water at the Sanctum. **Veyrin** — a sealer-scholar with no formal Order training — discovers this and tries, unsuccessfully, to maintain the binding alone. The work is killing him.

## YS 198 — The game opens

The player arrives in Ashenvale. Brenna posts a quest about wolves in Greenhollow. The player has no reason to know anything else is happening. The thread of the seal-failure is below the surface for the first hour of play and stays there, by design, until the player has unwound enough of the shape that it cannot stay hidden.

## Sources for new lore

- **Aelia Ren's journal pages** — scattered through dungeons. Read in order, they trace her arc from confident sealer at YS 0 to grieving cartographer at YS 12.
- **Yorath's notebook** — held in the Adventurer's Guild. Brenna offers it to the player after the revelation cutscene. Future content can let the player read it directly for additional canon.
- **Veyrin** — current keeper of the Drowned Sanctum seal. Source of contemporary information about how the bindings actually work.
- **Quill** — former Hollow King cultist, recruitable companion. Source of "what the Crown felt like to wear."
